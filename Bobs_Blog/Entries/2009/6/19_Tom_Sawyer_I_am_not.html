Friday found us stopping in Hannibal, Missouri, boyhood home of Mark Twain. The Quality Inn was a fine stop. The room was large and cold (it had been a very hot day, so the blast of air-conditioned cold was a welcomed relief).


Since Daniel is not a great traveler and prefers not to sight-see, our time in historic downtown Hannibal was brief. It’s a quaint little town, filled with antique shops and it sita at the edge of the Mississippi River.


The photo above is a dogs-eye view of a beautiful building on Main Street.


Our next stop is set for Omaha, Nebraska, home of Warren Buffet (though, I doubt we’ll spot him).


So far, we’re all holding up well. I’m glad we decided to do about 300 miles a day. That’s long enough with the 2 cars and the dog stops.
