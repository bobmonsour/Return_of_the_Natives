“Now he belongs to the ages.”


With Bob in the lead, our little caravan cris-crossed through residential streets of Springfield to Oak Ridge Cemetery where Abraham Lincoln is buried.


The grey stone and marble mausoleum sits on a little rise, and is quickly revealed as you make your way up the entrance road of the cemetery. It is very prominent, but still dignified.


The interior of the mausoleum is finished in earth-colored marble of varying hues; tans, browns, earth reds. A simple  corridor leads to the rear of the mausoleum. Several small bronze statues of Lincoln, each marking a different point in the great man’s life and career, are carefully placed at turns in the corridor. The interior environment is dignified and understated, perhaps that made the revelation of Lincoln’s simple tomb even more moving.
